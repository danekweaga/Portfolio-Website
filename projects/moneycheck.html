Summary

MoneyCheck converts a spending scenario into budget impact, opportunity cost, risk, future-value impact and a plain-language explanation grounded in calculated outputs.

My role and contribution

I designed and built the application end to end as the sole developer: product framing, data model, calculations, AI explanation layer, authentication, persistence and deployment.

Status

Live on Vercel. Source available on GitHub.

Technology

Next.js, React, TypeScript, Supabase, PostgreSQL, OpenAI, Vercel.

Problem

A purchase price rarely explains the full decision. People may need to understand how it affects their budget, savings goals and alternatives without working through every calculation themselves.

User workflow

To be documented — describe the steps a user takes from login or entry through scenario input, results and export.

What I personally built

  • Authentication and database-backed decision history.
  • Structured records for spending scenarios and calculated outputs.
  • API integrations and AI-generated explanations grounded in the calculated data.
  • A dashboard and exportable records.

Architecture or data flow

Deterministic financial calculations produce structured results. Those results are persisted and passed to an AI explanation step so the model describes computed outputs rather than inventing the analysis.

To be documented — diagram or more detailed request/response flow.

Engineering decisions

I kept deterministic calculations separate from the AI explanation. The model explains structured results instead of inventing the financial analysis, making the output easier to reason about and audit.
